Chapter 19: Annex

 

Hi all!
Once again, I promise that the MSAV is coming, I have quite the grand reveal planned for the coming weeks, so for all you folks who wanted to see the lander, a little patience will get you far. I wanted to take the time to highlight Jay's work and show off Minerva on her departure, as well as discuss a little bit more about the architecture of something like a nuclear thermal powered system. One of the advantages to building a nuclear powered system is the ability to abort fairly late into your journey, by burning all of your propellant back towards Earth. This would enable the crew to board their Earth Return Lifeboat and re-enter, albeit, sacrificing the MTV. In the Olympus Architecture, ultimately you want to reuse the core of the MTV as much as possible, and in the ideal mission architecture, the core stage parks very nicely in LEO after the whole mission is complete; ready to be refueled and refit with drop tanks. I wanted to bring this up to kind of dispel the myth that once you commit to Mars there is no way home, but it is not entirely without risk. But, returning to the story for a moment- the crew is well on their way, and I am so excited for what they have in store coming up, you all are gonna love it.


Minerva and Intrepid conduct joint operations in orbit, as the crews prepare for departure. Intrepid is now a veteran of two Olympus missions, with discussions underway to convert another orbiter for support capability.


Her mission complete, Intrepid undocks and conducts her flyaround, inspecting the MTV with human eyes for one last time before Trans Mars Injection.​


Minerva begins the Trans Mars Injection, pushing the crew of Olympus 3, and humanity itself, higher and faster than ever before.


Her Valkyrie nuclear engines glowing, Minerva continues the push into interplanetary space, conducting some of the most energy intensive maneuvers in human spaceflight.


With Trans Mars Injection complete, the crew can sit back and reflect on the sight of Earth growing distant, becoming a distant point of light among the cosmos.


Goodbye, Earth. Catch you on the flip side...
