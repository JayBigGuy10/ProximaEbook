Good morning all!, Today I wanted to showcase some incredible images from our good friend Jay, and talk about a new entry into our lineup of spacecraft/space stations, and why I think it will continue to be an important asset in Proxima for years to come. The Gateway Logistics station is a station designed to be mobile, designed to move around and rendezvous with different equipment in different orbits, and act as a servicing platform rather than a full time tended space station. The station is small, and built around a multifunctional node, enabling it to be reconfigured for a variety of different roles throughout its life (Maybe there's a little ODIN heritage in there, who knows). Gateway is equipped with a unique feature, an ion thruster assembly, to allow it to maneuver across a variety of orbits to service different payloads, and reach a variety of targets without spending a lot of fuel. Later additions will see tugs coupled with Gateway to enable more wide-reaching servicing. Potential applications that we'll see in Proxima include MTV servicing, satellite servicing, in space construction and more. Much of my inspiration for Gateway came from the real life Gateway being constructed for the Artemis program, and some came from the Exploration Platform that was proposed in the weird proto-Artemis stage circa 2012-2017. I wanted to showcase Gateway in its initial operating configuration, having been put together by Challenger in the Summer of 2008, and give you guys a taste of the future of Proxima. This is not our only new station to join the party, however, we have lots more to come.


The station largely ready to go, Gateway deploys her massive solar arrays, ready to start generating power for the facility and providing power to Challenger.


While the physical modules of the station are small, her solar arrays are required to be large in order to generate power for the ion drive, and power supplementary systems. This iconic "power tower" is derived from station concepts of old.


Her first expedition to Gateway complete, Challenger casts off, bidding adieu to NASA's latest home in orbit. Challenger would conduct a fly around, inspecting the station after her departure.


Ready for the next great leap in human spaceflight, Gateway awaits her next visit from the Shuttle, ready to get to work.
