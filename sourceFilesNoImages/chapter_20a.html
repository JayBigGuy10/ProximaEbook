Minerva's fall now nearly complete, she prepares herself for orbital insertion around the Red Planet, her drop tanks nearly depleted.


Minerva ditches her drop tanks as she performs orbital insertion, her comms issues only moments away. Despite a problematic insertion, Minerva would deliver her crew to a Martian orbit successfully.


Her orbit insertion complete, Minerva begins to make her way to the Mars Base Station, ready to meet the fleet already around the planet.


Docking with the Base Station complete, the crew begins to check out their home away from home. The Base Station has been relatively quiet, but her electronic eyes have been scanning the landing site of Elysium Planitia.


Zephyr loiters in Martian Orbit, having cast off its transfer stage. The vehicle's color scheme is designed to maximize visibility on the surface, enabling astronauts to wayfind back to the vehicle in the event of emergency.


Zephyr makes her approach to the Base Station, under Laurent St. Michel's watchful eye. The lander, having spent months in deep space, is no worse for wear, and performs the docking with ease, further expanding the Base Station complex.


Zephyr, as imaged from the Base Station complex during her uncrewed approach.
