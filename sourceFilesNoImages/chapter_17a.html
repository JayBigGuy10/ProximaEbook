Hi all!, I hope you all enjoyed our test flight of the Base Station and MTV systems, and look forward to the upcoming excitement. I wanted to highlight some more images from Jay today of the joint operations during Olympus 2, and share some thoughts about why I developed this architecture this way. When I first started development of the architecture, I noted a lack of contingency aborts in several mission architectures, such as the very rough Constellation system. As I researched, I found that the architecture of the Lockheed Martin Basecamp could be expanded upon, with your arrival station almost acting as a functional backup if something were to go wrong with your MTV. The Basecamp architecture also enables long loiter times in the planet's orbit, which allows for more time to correctly identify a landing site, operate telerobotic missions if needed, and plan your sortie to the surface with the upmost accuracy. Thus, I arrived at the Olympus Architecture: A destination and aggregation point, while still allowing for a functional backup if need be. I also want to apologize for no images of the MSAV, except for the depiction in the mission patch: she is a bit of a beast and I want to make sure that I can get the details absolutely perfect before I show it to you all.

With all that being said, let's take a look at some of the phenomenal images that Jay has put together for us!
